Drop off in the payment journey was at a staggering 45%. We had several theories as to why, and some bright spark had set up an abandon basket survey to collect responses, but no one had been brave enough to go through them. yet.

And after a day of wading through the swamp of 3000 responses, we had 5 distinct but secret categories to explore.

Exploring how we could tackle these areas. It became resoundingly clear that offering free sims, something commonplace amongst our competitors. Could drastically reduce drop off and help us win the customers we might otherwise lose. But there was the problem...

How could we, a humble team of 3. Create a way to test a free sim card offering, without overhauling our entire distribution and checkout flow?

Becoming the postman

Once the user details were in mailchimp, they would feed into the royal mail pre-postage service to make the labels. I would then add each to an envelope containing our trackable sim card and walk them to a nearby postbox.

Over the next 3 days I sent over 700 sim cards all over the UK. On the second day we saw a small uptick in activation, but had doubled total sim orders that day. By removing less commuted users from the funnel we saw an 8% increase in conversion. On the fourth day we received some heart breaking news. Black Friday was around the corner and we had to kill the test.

We were never able to reach statistical significance and it still keeps me up at night.
